About Me

I am a Web Developer getting back to what I love doing - coding. At my most recent job, I began as a Principal Software Engineer, and transitioned to an Engineering Manager role. I enjoy managing teams, but I found that I no longer had the time to continue coding too. I missed the feeling I got when I solved a particularly difficult problem, and the satisfaction I felt when a feature that I developed improved our users' experience in some way.
When the opportunity arose for me to leave my job and take some time for myself, I decided to attend the Web Development Immersive class at General Assembly to build my web development skills since my experience is primarily with server-side code. I am very interested in JavaScript in particular. It is a challenging language to learn, but it allows us to develop browser-based applications that are interactive and fun to use. It is well worth the effort it takes to learn to write good JavaScript code. I am bringing my computer science background, extensive software development experience, and commitment to writing high-quality, easily maintainable code to the world of JavaScript and Web Development.
